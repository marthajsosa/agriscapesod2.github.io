FAQ

Good questions, straight answers.

How much does sod cost in Florida?

It depends on square footage and grass variety. Use the instant estimate above for a ballpark, then request a free quote for exact, confirmed pricing — including free local delivery.

Do you deliver and install, or just supply?

Both. We grow, deliver, and install — including old turf removal and ground prep — or deliver fresh-cut sod by the pallet or truckload.

How fresh is the sod?

It's cut to order and harvested within 24 hours of delivery, so it arrives fresh and ready to root.

Do you handle large commercial and government projects?

Yes — we have the fleet and crews to serve HOAs, developers, contractors, and public infrastructure projects across Florida. See our Commercial & Government page for capacity details.

What areas do you serve?

Residential service covers Sarasota–Bradenton–Venice and Fort Myers–Cape Coral. Commercial and government projects extend to Tampa–St. Pete, Orlando, and the wider Southwest Florida region.

*Why the final installation price may be higher or lower This planning range assumes the measured area is reasonably accurate, the property is accessible to the crew and equipment, ordinary removal and soil preparation are sufficient, and no hidden condition requires added labor, equipment, material, disposal, repair, or delay.
  • Narrow gates, fenced yards, stairs, steep slopes, retaining walls, pools, canals, wet ground, or long distances that require sod, soil, or debris to be moved by hand.
  • Septic tanks, drain fields, wells, irrigation lines, utility lines, drainage systems, invisible fencing, wiring, or other underground features that limit equipment use or require hand work.
  • Thick existing turf, heavy roots, tree roots, stumps, rocks, concrete, pavers, buried debris, landscape fabric, or construction material that makes removal harder than expected.
  • Poor drainage, standing water, erosion, severe soil compaction, unsuitable soil, low areas, uneven grades, or the need for imported soil, fill, sand, grading, drainage correction, or erosion control.
  • Sprinkler repairs, head relocation, missing irrigation coverage, pressure issues, damaged lines, or irrigation adjustments before or after installation.
  • Trees, landscape beds, patios, driveways, walkways, curbing, playgrounds, sheds, furniture, pet areas, and other obstacles that increase detailed cutting or reduce installation speed.
  • Extra hauling or disposal, restricted parking, traffic control, gated access, HOA requirements, permits, site rules, limited work hours, or special cleanup requirements.
  • Sod variety and availability, farm conditions, fuel and delivery costs, job minimums, equipment needs, weather, saturated soil, drought, heat, crew scheduling, or changes requested by the customer.
  • Differences between aerial, customer-entered, and verified square footage, especially where trees, shadows, old imagery, pools, additions, beds, or recent grading obscure the lawn boundary.
  • Conditions discovered during the free on-site review or after removal begins that could not reasonably be confirmed from the visible surface, photos, measurements, or aerial imagery.
